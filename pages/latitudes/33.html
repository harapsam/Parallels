As we venture further north in Morocco, we encounter regions that exhibit increasingly dense human habitation. Morocco's eastern expansion is curtailed by the vast expanse of the sea, and as a result, a significant majority of the population gravitates toward the western regions, mirroring the natural contours of the coastline. When we continue our northern journey, hugging the western edge as closely as possible, one prominent destination comes into focus: Casablanca.


A visual representation of Moroccan cities with populations exceeding 1,000 is presented in the graph below, but it proves challenging to discern meaningful patterns. The graph employs the x-axis to measure population in millions, yet it remains somewhat incomprehensible due to a striking outlier: Casablanca. It's the orange dot all the way on the right of the graph. This dynamic city significantly deviates from the norm, pushing all other towns and municipalities into a tight cluster along the y-axis, with populations ranging from 0 to 1,000,000 inhabitants.


In stark contrast, Casablanca extends its boundaries far beyond this constrained range, boasting a population that surpasses the 4,000,000 mark. This extraordinary demographic divergence positions Casablanca as a significant outlier within Morocco's urban landscape, challenging conventional expectations and illustrating the unique character of this coastal metropolis.

The significance of Casablanca in the context of demographic analysis within Morocco is a well-established and enduring phenomenon. Casablanca has played a pivotal role in the country's demographic landscape, a role that dates back to colonial times when the visionary French urbanist Henri Prost envisioned it as the "New York of North Africa." This foresight has had a lasting impact on the city's development and demographic trends.


A compelling insight arises when we delve into population data spanning from 1950 to the present day: the city's population growth exhibits a strikingly consistent pattern, resembling a linear trajectory at a 45-degree angle on a graph. In this context, a slope of one signifies a consistent population increase of 500,000 residents approximately every ten years. This remarkable continuity underscores the stability and reliability of Casablanca's population growth, making it a standout case in demographic analyses not only in Morocco but also on a global scale.





This robust trend appears to be deeply rooted in the city's socio-economic dynamics, historical significance, and strategic geographic location. Casablanca's allure as a center for commerce, industry, and opportunity has drawn people from various parts of Morocco and beyond. Moreover, the city's climatic conditions and available economic opportunities continue to attract individuals and families, contributing to its ongoing population expansion.


When we consider the available data on population density and climatic factors, there are no immediate indications or projections suggesting a deviation from this established growth trajectory in the foreseeable future. Casablanca's unique status as a demographic outlier within Morocco exemplifies the enduring allure of this dynamic city and the consistent opportunities it offers to those who call it home.


When we examine the percentage of the Casablanca population divided by ethnic groups, it becomes clear how, despite massive internal migration of Moroccans displaced from rural areas after losing their land and in search of work, foreigners made up a significant portion of the city's population during the first part of the 20th century. After gaining independence, the number of foreigners decreased. As of 2023, the number of immigrant residents in Casablanca is minimal, as is the Jewish population, which largely emigrated to Israel after the country's creation in 1948.

Indeed, the city of Casablanca is experiencing a surge in construction activity. However, it's crucial to delve deeper into the nature of these ongoing developments. With the assistance of data provided by the Moroccan government, we can gain a more nuanced understanding. Notably, the preponderance of construction efforts in Casablanca is directed towards the creation of affordable, low-income housing. This observation carries significant implications, pointing towards the city's continuous response to the pressing issue of accommodating underprivileged and displaced individuals. Analyzing this data in conjunction with the previously examined pie chart detailing the ethnic composition of the population, it becomes evident that the influx of foreign migrants from other African or Middle Eastern countries does not constitute a substantial proportion of Casablanca's residents. Instead, the majority of these construction projects are likely aimed at addressing the needs of displaced people originating from rural communities within Morocco itself. This underscores the city's ongoing efforts to provide housing and opportunities for its own marginalized population, reflecting Casablanca's role as a hub for socioeconomic development within the country.
