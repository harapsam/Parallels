"Tiwizi" is an Amazigh term that translates to "mutual assistance" in English. This concept of mutual assistance is deeply rooted in Amazigh culture and represents the practice of community members coming together to support each other in various tasks and activities.


It is a concept they rely on when they have tasks or projects that require more labor than can be provided by a single family, such as agricultural activities like planting, harvesting, or other communal work. In such cases, the community members collaborate and pool their resources to get the job done efficiently.


Households work together to collectively and successively harvest all their fields. This means that instead of each family working on their own, they coordinate their efforts to ensure that all of the are used and gathered for the benefit of everyone. This cooperative approach not only helps with the completion of labor-intensive tasks but also fosters a sense of unity and solidarity among community members.
